# Individual Games Guide

Individual games are daily solo challenges designed to sharpen your sourcing skills across multiple categories. Each game tests specific competencies and provides AI-powered feedback to help you improve.

## How Individual Games Work

### Daily Rotation System

- **New Game Daily**: A fresh challenge appears each day
- **Game Cycle**: Games rotate through a curated collection
- **Always Available**: Previous games remain accessible - you can play any game at any time
- **Unlimited Attempts**: Retry games as many times as you want to improve your score

### Game Structure

Each game consists of:

1. **Title**: Descriptive name of the challenge (e.g., "The Boolean Blacksmith")
2. **Description**: Scenario and context for the challenge
3. **Task**: Specific instructions on what you need to create or solve
4. **Difficulty Level**: Easy, Medium, or Hard
5. **Skill Category**: The primary skill being tested
6. **Submission Box**: Where you craft your solution
7. **Placeholder Text**: Example format to guide your submission

## Scoring System

### How Scoring Works

1. **AI Evaluation**: Your submission is analyzed by Gemini AI
2. **Rubric Application**: Each criterion is scored individually
3. **Total Calculation**: Points are summed for your final score (0-100)
4. **Feedback Generation**: Detailed feedback explains your score
5. **Best Score Saved**: Your highest attempt is recorded

### Score Interpretation

- **90-100**: Excellent - Expert-level execution
- **80-89**: Very Good - Strong understanding with minor improvements possible
- **70-79**: Good - Solid foundation with room for refinement
- **60-69**: Fair - Meets basic requirements but needs improvement
- **50-59**: Below Average - Significant gaps in execution
- **0-49**: Needs Work - Fundamental issues to address

## AI Coach Feedback

Every submission receives detailed feedback including overall assessment, criteria breakdown, strengths, areas for improvement, and actionable tips.

## Strategies for High Scores

**Q: Do games expire?**
A: No, all games remain available indefinitely.
