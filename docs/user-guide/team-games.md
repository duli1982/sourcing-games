# Team Games Guide

Team games are collaborative bi-weekly challenges designed for teams to work together on advanced multi-platform sourcing strategies.

## How Team Games Work

### Bi-Weekly Rotation Schedule

- **15th of Each Month**: New team game released
- **End of Each Month**: Another team game released
- **24 Total Games**: Rotating collection of challenges
- **One Submission Per Team**: Teams submit collaboratively

### Team Game Structure

Each team game includes:

1. Title and description
2. Multi-part task requirements
3. Difficulty level and skill category
4. Platform focus (GitHub, Stack Overflow, Reddit)
5. Submission requirements

## Skill Categories

### Multi-Platform Sourcing (9 games)

Master sourcing across GitHub, Stack Overflow, and Reddit with platform-specific strategies.

### Boolean Search (6 games)

Advanced Boolean challenges requiring team collaboration and complex search strategies.

### Outreach Strategy (6 games)

Collaborative outreach planning including multi-channel campaigns and personalization at scale.

### Talent Intelligence (3 games)

Strategic market analysis and sourcing recommendations with team coordination.

## Team Submission Process

1. **Team Discussion**: Review challenge together
2. **Divide Responsibilities**: Assign tasks to team members
3. **Research & Draft**: Each member works on their part
4. **Team Review**: Collaborate to refine submission
5. **Final Submission**: One member submits for the team

### Who Can Submit

- Any active team member can submit
- Submission is attributed to the submitter
- Score applies to entire team
- Best score per game is recorded

## Scoring System

### Team Game Rubrics

**Easy (100 points):**
- Team Strategy (30 points)
- Core Requirements (25 points)
- Platform Understanding (25 points)
- Completeness (20 points)

**Medium (100 points):**
- Advanced Team Strategy (25 points)
- Strategic Depth (30 points)
- Platform Expertise (25 points)
- Innovation & Optimization (20 points)

**Hard (100 points):**
- Expert Team Coordination (25 points)
- Comprehensive Strategy (30 points)
- Advanced Platform Mastery (25 points)
- Strategic Excellence (20 points)

## Team Games Leaderboard

Teams are ranked by sum of best scores across all team games. This is separate from the main team leaderboard which tracks individual game scores.

## Strategies for Success

### Team Coordination

- Schedule meetings to review challenges
- Assign roles based on strengths
- Use shared documents
- Regular progress check-ins
- Peer review before submission

### Platform-Specific Tips

**GitHub:**
- Use advanced search syntax
- Evaluate contribution quality
- Check activity recency
- Review repository impact

**Stack Overflow:**
- Filter by tags and reputation
- Assess teaching ability
- Check answer recency
- Evaluate expertise breadth

**Reddit:**
- Identify relevant subreddits
- Evaluate engagement quality
- Look for thought leadership
- Consider community reputation

### Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Poor coordination between members
- Inconsistent quality across sections
- Generic strategies without platform specifics
- Missing required elements
- No team review process
- Ignoring previous feedback

## Managing Team Submissions

### Setting Your Active Team

1. Navigate to Teams page
2. Select desired team
3. Click Set as Active Team
4. Submissions count for this team

### Viewing Team Progress

Check your team detail page for:
- Total team game score
- Games played
- Recent attempts
- Member contributions
- Team ranking

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can team members work on individual games separately?**
A: Yes! Individual and team games are independent.

**Q: Can we change our submission after submitting?**
A: Yes, submit multiple times. Best score is recorded.

**Q: What if a team member is inactive?**
A: Team admins can manage membership.

**Q: Do we get separate feedback per member?**
A: No, feedback is for the team submission as a whole.
